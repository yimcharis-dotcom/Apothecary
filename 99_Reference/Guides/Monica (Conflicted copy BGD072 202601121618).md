   - **Practical Tips**:
     - **Standardized Formats**:
       - Develop templates for different types of notes (projects, prompts, reviews). This ensures consistency and saves time.

         ```markdown
         # Project Title

         - Description of the project objective.
         ## Steps
         - Step 1: Description
         - Step 2: Description
         ```

   - **Practical Tips**:
     - **When to Jot**:
       - Capture notes whenever you encounter new information—during meetings, while reading articles, or when attending webinars. Don’t wait for a dedicated time; jot down ideas as they come.

       - Key concepts, interesting quotes, questions that arise, and your thoughts on the material. Use bullet points for clarity and brevity.

         ```markdown
         # Note Title

         - Concept 1: Explanation
         - Concept 2: Explanation
         ## Questions
         - Question 1
         - Question 2
         ## Personal Thoughts
         - Your reflections on the material.
         ```
     - **Contextual Notes**:
       - Provide context in your notes. For instance, if you learn a new AI concept, note where you learned it (source), why it’s relevant, and how it connects to your other interests.

       - Use tags to categorize notes across different domains. For example, tag notes with #AI, #Audit, #Accounting. This allows for easy cross-referencing.
     - **Link Related Notes**:
       - Whenever you mention a concept from another note, create a link to it. For example, if discussing AI in accounting, link to both the AI concepts and accounting principles notes.
     - **Map of Content (MOC)**:
